An original online game where players compete to strike first. I'm especially proud of this project since I built it entirely from the ground up. In the process, I learned the basics of HTML/CSS and grew a deep appreciation for JavaScript. Extensive debugging and logging helped me become much more comfortable with its quirks. All the assets you see in game, from the sounds to the player animations, are original. I used Krita and Photoshop to animate/draw sprites and organise them on a spritesheet when needed. For sound design I utilised FL Studio 21.

Let's explore some of the thought process that went into the design of some assets.

For sound design in particular, I had a few objectives. I wanted sounds to feel natural, I wanted sounds to feel satisfying and I wanted to ensure that sounds didn't get old when played frequently. Here was a quick and effective solution I implemented in order to meet my third objective: For frequently used sounds in the game I created slight variations of the original sound (slightly pitched up/down, delay/reverb adjusted slightly), which made the sound feel a bit different. Similarly when designing player animations I also had a set of objectives in mind. Like my sounds, I wanted my animations to feel satisfying, and accent the fast-pace of the game. Throughout my process I also thought profoundly about how information is communicated to the player through visuals. When players are performing important actions like attacking and parrying- they should be clear and recognisable. I accomplished this in a few ways- some very clear and some more subtle.

During an attack, the entire character assumes the colour of their swords- clearly indicating that they've become an active hitbox (i.e., they're currently attacking):


There is no other state where the player completely changes colour like this- and that's an intentional decision to ensure that players instantly associate that colour change with an active attack.

Another action which can be performed in the game is a parry, these require anticipating an enemy attack and aren't easy to perform correctly. For this reason I sought to ensure that landing a parry felt rewarding. On the visual front, I used a subtle colour trick to make parries feel much more vibrant. Normally when the character is swinging around their swords- there are traces which simulate the sword's movement. During parrying, these traces brighten all the way up, turning to solid white. Rentify

This full stack application was a developped using Java and XML in Android Studio. It was a major excercise in software engineering concepts, as well as both front-end and back-end development. In this project, I was tasked with creating an app where renters could create/manage postings for items and lessors could access those postings. To implement everything in our app from accounts to postings to rentings, I made use of Firebase realtime database.


Across the application asynchronous methods were used to communicate with Firebase. We additionally used them to dynamically update the UI, sending the user feedback based on what was happening on the server.
